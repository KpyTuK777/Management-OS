# Perceptual Architecture

## Purpose and status

This document defines how the primary Operational Matter workspace makes the
roles of the Board, owner contribution, and Watson recognizable before detailed
reading.

## Canonical model

The Operational Matter workspace has three canonical surfaces:

1. **Operational Situation Board — Board shows.** It projects the current
   operational picture and remains the atmospheric center.
2. **Natural Capture — owner contributes.** It receives and preserves the owner's
   original contribution through a neutral, predictable working tool.
3. **Watson Interaction Surface — Watson interacts.** It contains active AI
   inquiry, recommendation, classification, warning, explanation, and requests
   for owner judgment.

Each surface has one role, one speaker, and one perceptual character. Their
distinction must be apparent from position, composition, treatment, and
affordance—not color or explanatory labels alone.

## Operational Situation Board

The Board visualizes understanding rather than artifact inventory. It makes
condition, Matter state, current understanding, material Evidence, contradiction,
uncertainty, relationship, change, Decision Readiness, and relevant execution or
outcome signals perceptible.

Controlled visual intelligence may include compact trends, comparisons,
directional indicators, relationship lines, provenance and freshness signals,
contradiction markers, Inflection Point emphasis, warnings, and qualitative
Evidence contribution. Every element must answer what changed, compared with
what, why it matters, how understanding changed, or what remains uncertain.

Beauty supports investigation by creating focus, curiosity, memorability, and
return value. It comes from composition, relationships, restraint, and meaningful
change—not decorative dashboards, theatrical detective props, or dense card
inventories.

## Natural Capture

Natural Capture is the owner's low-friction working zone. It favors direct input,
predictable behavior, attachments, paste, optional structure, voice where
available, and visible preservation of original wording.

It contains owner contribution and tool state. Watson classification,
explanation, and consequential review occur in the Watson Surface. Submission
does not replace or rewrite the owner's original contribution.

## Watson Interaction Surface

Every active Watson intervention uses one recognizable surface with stable
identity and contextual connection. The exact form may be a desktop side surface,
tablet drawer, mobile sheet, or bounded contextual overlay; the contract remains
the same.

The surface presents one dominant Guided Investigation interaction and may
temporarily replace it with clarification, classification, contradiction,
Evidence, approval, or warning review. It exposes why now, what it seeks, and what
may change. The owner can answer, redirect, defer, reject, or inspect alternatives.

When Watson references a Board element, that source remains inspectable and may
receive temporary emphasis. Watson guides attention without duplicating the
Board. Material risk, contradiction, uncertainty, authority, and required owner
judgment remain visible outside a closed Watson Surface.

## Cross-surface visual language

| Attribute | Board | Natural Capture | Watson Surface |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Character | Composed operational picture | Neutral professional tool | Active bounded partner |
| Background | Atmospheric, low elevation | Quiet inset work surface | Distinct raised surface |
| Density | Selective synthesis | Minimal | Concise interaction |
| Primary affordance | Inspect | Contribute | Respond or judge |
| Motion | Material change only | Submission feedback | Restrained intervention arrival |
| Identity | Matter and operational signals | Owner-authored input | Stable Watson identity |

The surfaces share typography, spacing, tokens, accessibility, and component
quality so differentiation does not fragment product identity.

## Guided Investigation and responsive behavior

On desktop, the Board remains the main column, Natural Capture is directly
available within the owner work path, and Watson occupies a persistent secondary
surface. Tablet may collapse Watson into a stable drawer. Mobile keeps the Board
as the default view and uses explicitly identified Capture and Watson modes.

Responsive adaptation never turns the workspace into one undifferentiated feed,
makes chat primary, or hides consequence-bearing Board conditions. Capability
availability and source authority do not change across layouts.

## Cognitive cleanup

Permanently visible information is limited to Matter identity and state, current
understanding, dominant uncertainty, material risk or contradiction, Natural
Capture access, and Watson's one current interaction.

Contextual presentation includes requested Evidence, current comparisons,
classification proposals, and temporarily referenced Board elements. Complete
timeline, reasoning history, provenance, alternatives, Memory, Laboratory,
Investigation history, and Knowledge remain inspectable.

Repeated authority reminders, duplicate guidance, architectural terminology,
identical treatment for different roles, and simultaneous primary calls to action
do not belong in the primary view.

## Validation

The perceptual architecture succeeds when an owner can identify, before reading:

- the current operational picture;
- where to contribute what they know;
- when Watson is actively interacting;
- which single action deserves attention.

The deterministic prototype demonstrates presentation and page-memory interaction
only. It adds no source authority, persistence, production AI, visualization
engine, attachment processing, voice capability, or adaptive layout runtime.
