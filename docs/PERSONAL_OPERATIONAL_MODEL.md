# Personal Operational Model

## Purpose and status

The Personal Operational Model is the future owner-controlled source of durable,
explicitly approved understanding about how the owner works and receives
assistance.

## Ownership

The owner owns the Personal Operational Model. The Assistant may retrieve approved
context and propose additions or corrections, but it cannot establish durable
personal understanding through conversation history, passive behavior, silence,
postponement, or a single event.

Durable content exists only after explicit, bounded owner approval and remains
inspectable, correctable, narrowable, revocable, and deletable.

## Eligible content

Subject to separate privacy, retention, and security governance, the future model
may preserve:

- approved explanation, planning, review, or clarification preferences;
- approved primary and secondary Operating Contexts;
- at most one durable primary Operating Context;
- an optional Operational Stage within a context;
- owner-defined effective periods or review dates;
- approved Workspace Emphasis preferences;
- explicit corrections, limitations, and revocation state.

Current Focus normally belongs to active operational or working context rather
than durable personal learning. It may reference an Operating Context without
becoming a permanent preference.

Current Focus may also reference an Operational Case, but Case content, lifecycle,
and relationships remain operational records rather than Personal Operational
Model beliefs. Case authority remains defined by the
[Operational Case Architecture](OPERATIONAL_CASE_ARCHITECTURE.md).

Case results must not become hidden judgments about the owner's competence,
performance, or problem-solving quality. Repeated Case interaction may support an
explicit coaching-format or explanation preference recommendation, never a durable
performance profile.

Operational Learning performance, training scores, Decision Laboratory history, and modeled
outcomes also remain outside this model. Gym may create Training History and
Simulation may create Scenario History, but a future Owner Learning Record or
Operational Capability Development Model must govern durable capability
development. Only explicitly approved preferences or context may enter the
Personal Operational Model.

## Prohibited content and inference

The Personal Operational Model must not silently contain or infer psychological,
personality, health, or performance profiles; authorization or organizational
authority; organizational facts or procedure truth; a durable context from job
title, passive navigation, or isolated work; predictions presented as identity or
fact; or hidden scoring that changes capability access.

Operating Context describes the owner's current operating environment. It is not
the owner's identity.

## Approval lifecycle

```text
Explicit owner statement or authorized Evidence
  -> Temporary interpretation
  -> Optional recommendation to remember or change
  -> Consequence preview
  -> Explicit owner approval
  -> Durable Personal Operational Model entry
```

Approval identifies the exact belief or context, intended use, duration where
applicable, affected interactions, and correction or revocation path. Approval to
use temporary session context is not approval to remember it durably.

Repeated consistent behavior may support a recommendation to learn; it never
creates the durable entry automatically. A rejected or revoked proposal performs
no alternative learning.

## Precedence

```text
Canonical rules
  > Authoritative domain records
  > Current explicit owner instruction
  > Active temporary context
  > Approved Personal Operational Model entry
  > Working interpretation or AI inference
```

A preference or Operating Context may shape relevance and presentation but cannot
override terminology, Evidence, confidence, validation, governance, or approval.

## Multiple Operating Contexts

Multiple approved Operating Contexts may coexist because an owner may be learning,
employed, operating a business, and researching simultaneously. At most one is the
durable primary context used as the default input to Workspace Emphasis.

Selecting a temporary active context does not replace the durable primary context.
Removing or expiring the primary context leaves the model with no primary context
until the owner explicitly selects another; the workspace then uses neutral
emphasis.

## Relationship with AI and Adaptive Workspace

The Assistant may use an approved entry only when relevant and authorized. It must
make a material personalization effect explainable, expose conflict with current
wording, and prefer the current explicit instruction.

The Personal Operational Model does not generate or apply workspace changes. An
evidence-based change belongs to the Recommendations Center and requires owner
approval before Adaptive Workspace application.

## Inspection and degraded operation

The owner must be able to inspect what is remembered, why it was added, how it is
used, and whether it is active; then correct, narrow, revoke, or delete it.

When AI is unavailable, approved deterministic context may remain readable and may
continue to support an already approved Workspace Emphasis. No new durable entry
is inferred. If context is missing, invalid, expired, or revoked, the workspace
uses neutral emphasis.

## Boundary

The Personal Operational Model is personal operational context, not profiling,
Organizational Knowledge, Session Memory, Working Memory, an AI conversation log,
or an authority source. This architecture defines no current storage schema,
retention period, API, model, runtime, or user interface. Those require separate
approved architecture and implementation work.

It is also not [Operational Memory](OPERATIONAL_MEMORY_ARCHITECTURE.md).
Experience Patterns describe recurring organizational behavior across authorized
Operational Episodes; Personal Operational Model entries describe only approved
personal assistance context. Neither may silently populate or override the other.
