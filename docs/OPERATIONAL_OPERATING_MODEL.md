## Definition

The **Operational Operating Model** is a governed representation of how the
organization operates. It connects structure with processes, systems, roles,
handoffs, controls, constraints, decision rights, Evidence access, and relevant
operating characteristics so Management OS can reason about current conditions
and proposed change without reducing the organization to a hierarchy.

Eligible context may include:

- organizational units, teams, responsibilities, roles, decision rights, reporting,
  and escalation relationships;
- operational processes, process steps, dependencies, capacity points, and handoffs;
- systems, integrations, information flows, and appropriately governed access;
- SOPs, controls, measures, monitoring expectations, and operating standards;
- working hours, department availability, interview windows, and reporting cadence;
- declared communication preferences and expected response ranges;
- system adoption, availability, and digital maturity at an appropriately governed
  organizational level;
- operational constraints, recurring bottlenecks, travel requirements, Evidence
  availability, and department-specific operating notes.

Its purpose is shared operational topology for backward Investigation reasoning
and forward change-impact reasoning, not organization-chart administration,
employee scoring, surveillance, automatic judgment about people, or autonomous
prediction.

## Process understanding projections

An Investigation may progressively project what it currently understands about a
referenced process or process step:

- **Not explored**
- **Under investigation**
- **Suspected concern**
- **Contradiction detected**
- **Confirmed bottleneck**
- **Excluded**
- **Unresolved**

These are Investigation-owned epistemic projections over Operating Model
references. They are not operational performance, compliance, quality, maturity,
or employee ratings and never rewrite the live process record.

Each material projection preserves the Investigation, referenced process or step,
supporting and contradictory Evidence, accepted conclusions, limitations,
relevant dependencies and handoffs, actor, time, and applicable decision-time
snapshot. Completed Investigations may retain this process understanding map when
it materially influenced a Decision. The Operating Model supplies stable identity
and topology; the Investigation retains authority for what it understood.

## Authority and relationship boundaries

The model is a supporting capability, not part of the Investigation or Matter
aggregate and not Organizational Knowledge, Operational Memory, or the Personal
Operational Model. Source systems retain authority. Investigations, Impact
Reviews, and plans reference authorized current context and may freeze a
decision-relevant snapshot without rewriting the source.

Every material item preserves source, scope, authorization, sensitivity, freshness,
and correction path. Declared information is distinguished from observed behavior
and inferred characteristics. Individual-level behavioral inference, hidden
performance profiling, or expansion of access is prohibited without separately
approved architecture and authority.

AI may identify missing, stale, or conflicting operating context and propose a
correction or collection action. It may not silently create, infer, approve, or
change durable operating facts, process projections, or relationships.

## Capability use

- **Operational Investigation** uses the model to assess Evidence access,
  feasibility, dependencies, handoffs, and operating context while preserving
  Investigation-owned process-understanding projections.
- **Operational Impact Review** uses the model to identify potentially affected
  processes, systems, roles, SOPs, controls, measures, dependencies, and
  organizational functions. The review interprets consequences; the model does
  not predict or approve them.
- **Execution Planning** receives only owner-approved adaptations and uses current
  topology to plan scope, sequence, ownership, coordination, and validation.
- **Operational Memory** may use governed topology references to compare completed
  episodes, but recurrence never becomes current truth or authority to act.

The model may inform feasibility, sequencing, expected delay, communication
channel, Evidence accessibility, and planning proposals. It never establishes a
Cause, overrides current Evidence, authorizes contact, reserves time, assigns
work, approves an adaptation, or determines a Decision. Degraded operation exposes
uncertainty and allows manual reasoning without pretending missing context is
known.
